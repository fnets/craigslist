Club sifts through the ever-expanding world of podcasts and recommends 10–15 of the previous week’s best episodes. Have your own favorite? Let us know in the comments or at <a href="mailto:podmass@avclub.com">podmass@avclub.com</a>.

      <div class="podmass-entry">
        <h4>
          <span class="podmass-entry-podcast">
            Comedy Bang! Bang!
          </span>
          <br>
          <span class="podmass-entry-episode">
            <a href="http://www.earwolf.com/episode/the-7th-anniversary-show/" data-track-action="Podmass Article: Episode" data-track-label="http://www.earwolf.com/episode/the-7th-anniversary-show/" target="_blank">
              7th Anniversary: Jason Mantzoukas, Paul F. Tompkins, Horatio Sanz, Neil Campbell, Mike Hanford, Tim Baltz, Drew Tarver
            </a>
          </span>
        </h4>
        <div class="podmass-entry-image">
          <figure>
    <div data-type="image" class="image crop-1x1" data-image-id="525923" data-rendered="true">
        <div>
            <noscript>
                &lt;img src="http://i.onionstatic.com/avclub/5259/23/1x1/150.jpg" &gt;
            </noscript>
        <img src="./Mostly Lit drinks the Lemonade, discusses Warsan Shire and Beyoncé · Podmass · The A.V. Club_files/200(4).jpg"></div>
    </div>

    

    
</figure>
          
          <a class="itunes-link" href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/comedy-bang-bang-the-podcast/id316045799?mt=2&amp;uo=4&amp;at=10lPNj" data-track-action="Podmass Article: iTunes" data-track-label="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/comedy-bang-bang-the-podcast/id316045799?mt=2&amp;uo=4&amp;at=10lPNj" target="_blank">
            <img src="./Mostly Lit drinks the Lemonade, discusses Warsan Shire and Beyoncé · Podmass · The A.V. Club_files/itunes-subscribe.svg">
          </a>
          
        </div>
        <div class="podmass-entry-text">
          <p><i>Comedy Bang! Bang!</i> celebrates its seventh anniversary by giving listeners an episode piled high with fan favorites—from classics like Horatio Sanz as Shelly Driftwood, to newer characters like Mike Hanford’s John Lennon—all of which are zany and charming in their own ways. In particular, John Lennon, who Hanford plays as a bumbling idiot with nothing but nice things to say, is a bright and refreshing character who pairs well with <i>Comedy Bang! Bang</i>!’s mainstays. Playing to his comedic peak, Scott Aukerman baits Shelly Driftwood to give exhaustingly detailed driving directions, which end up working as the highlight reel of the character. Also in on the fun are Maxwell Keeper with exasperated cadence and perfect timing and Neil Campbell with a strong and spontaneous delivery. It’s an endlessly entertaining episode that showcases some of the most lovable character avenues <i>Comedy Bang! Bang! </i>has carved out, and it’s the perfect way to celebrate seven hilarious years.</p><div class="dfp dfp-slot-inread " data-ad-unit="inread">
</div>
        </div>
        <div class="podmass-entry-author">
          [<span class="author"><a class="author" href="http://www.avclub.com/author/rebeccabulnez/" data-track-action="Podmass Article: Author" data-track-label="/author/rebeccabulnez/">Rebecca Bulnes</a></span>]
        </div>
      </div>
      
      <div class="podmass-entry">
        <h4>
          <span class="podmass-entry-podcast">
            Design Matters
          </span>
          <br>
          <span class="podmass-entry-episode">
            <a href="http://designobserver.com/feature/nico-muhly/39262" data-track-action="Podmass Article: Episode" data-track-label="http://designobserver.com/feature/nico-muhly/39262" target="_blank">
              Nico Muhly
            </a>
          </span>
        </h4>
        <div class="podmass-entry-image">
          <figure>
    <div data-type="image" class="image crop-1x1" data-image-id="575522" data-rendered="true">
        <div>
            <noscript>
                &lt;img src="http://i.onionstatic.com/avclub/5755/22/1x1/150.jpg" &gt;
            </noscript>
        <img src="./Mostly Lit drinks the Lemonade, discusses Warsan Shire and Beyoncé · Podmass · The A.V. Club_files/200(5).jpg"></div>
    </div>

    

    
</figure>
          
          <a class="itunes-link" href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/design-matters-debbie-millman/id328074695?mt=2&amp;uo=4&amp;at=10lPNj" data-track-action="Podmass Article: iTunes" data-track-label="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/design-matters-debbie-millman/id328074695?mt=2&amp;uo=4&amp;at=10lPNj" target="_blank">
            <img src="./Mostly Lit drinks the Lemonade, discusses Warsan Shire and Beyoncé · Podmass · The A.V. Club_files/itunes-subscribe.svg">
          </a>
          
        </div>
        <div class="podmass-entry-text">
          <p>During this wholly interesting episode of <i>Design Matters</i>, listeners can’t help shake the suspicion that celebrated contemporary classical composer Nico Muhly is actually a character from a Wes Anderson film come to life. Perhaps it’s in the quiet grandeur of his profession—Muhly is easily the most well-known classical composer in decades—or in his upbringing as the son of a painter and a documentary filmmaker, or perhaps it is his quirky fascination with the development history of ligature sorts used in frontispiece printing. It mostly comes through in Muhly’s enthusiastically erudite discussions of musicology, including his obsession with Steve Reich and opera, especially when one considers that he is still rather young at 34. The best moment comes when Muhly injects a bit of pedestrian relatability into the conversation, explaining to host Debbie Millman that his critically lauded opera <i>Two Boys</i> shares as much in common with Mozart’s<i> Così Fan Tutte </i>as it does with <i>Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</i>. The conversation between the two is chock-full of similarly engaging moments, giving listeners a window into a world they are otherwise not privy to; another wonderful listen from this most assured of podcasts.</p>
        </div>
        <div class="podmass-entry-author">
          [<span class="author"><a class="author" href="http://www.avclub.com/author/cannonfire/" data-track-action="Podmass Article: Author" data-track-label="/author/cannonfire/">Ben Cannon</a></span>]
        </div>

            <a href="http://feedproxy.google.com/~r/dinnerpartydownload/~3/VnZBC39JjoM/dpd_20160429_128.mp3" data-track-action="Podmass Article: Episode" data-track-label="http://feedproxy.google.com/~r/dinnerpartydownload/~3/VnZBC39JjoM/dpd_20160429_128.mp3" target="_blank">
              Jessica Williams, Dick Cavett, Michael McKean
            </a>
          </span>
        </h4>
        <div class="podmass-entry-image">
          <figure>
    <div data-type="image" class="image crop-1x1" data-image-id="580945" data-rendered="true">
        <div>
            <noscript>
                &lt;img src="http://i.onionstatic.com/avclub/5809/45/1x1/150.jpg" &gt;
            </noscript>

</figure>
          
          <a class="itunes-link" href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-dinner-party-download/id289105827?mt=2&amp;uo=4&amp;at=10lPNj" data-track-action="Podmass Article: iTunes" data-track-label="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-dinner-party-download/id289105827?mt=2&amp;uo=4&amp;at=10lPNj" target="_blank">
            <img src="./Mostly Lit drinks the Lemonade, discusses Warsan Shire and Beyoncé · Podmass · The A.V. Club_files/itunes-subscribe.svg">
          </a>
          
        </div>
        <div class="podmass-entry-text">
          <p><i>The Dinner Party Download</i> has fast paced segments that keep listeners interested in everything they need to survive the dinner party conversations of a given week. Hosts Rico Gagliano and Brendan Francis Newnam navigate us from the subjects of history and food to an excerpt from Jen Kirkman’s new book <i>I Know What I’m Doing—And Other Lies I Tell Myself. </i>Kirkman reads a piece that involves blackmail and a relationship on the rocks with a skillful level of emotion and nuance, but the crux of the episode is the hosts’ live conversation with Jessica Williams and Dick Cavett. Cavett, who has probably talked to every prominent figure since 1968, is asked to describe his thought process as an interviewer. When talking to Williams, Gagliano and Newnam unveil an aspect of her career that she has tried to keep hidden: a co-starring role in a 2006 Nickelodeon kids show <i>Just For Kicks</i>. Their third party guest, Michael McKean, describes his shift from playing comedic characters to those of a villainous nature. This podcast downloads an astonishing amount of information into the brains of its listeners, and the most important piece of advice for dinner party survival comes from Dick Cavett. His response on how to navigate interviews is, “Try to listen to what the guest is saying.” </p>

        </div>

            Doughboys

        <div class="podmass-entry-text">
          <p>That neither Lapkus nor the temporary host know what to expect is what makes <i>With Special Guest Lauren Lapkus</i> exciting. The genuine unpredictability of this week’s episode, for example, finds Chris Gethard hosting his actual Earwolf show <i>Beautiful Stories from Anonymous People</i> (slightly altered into <i>Anonymous Stories From Beautiful People</i>), with Lapkus acting as the anonymous caller. Gethard plays the entire episode with the utmost sincerity. Lapkus plays a caller whose father left home because of, as it’s slowly revealed, the many convoluted pranks her character put him through. As in actual episodes of the show, Gethard engages with the caller intimately and hardly ever makes it about himself, which allows him to push Lapkus for details, prompting her to come up with increasingly ridiculous specifics about the torment she put her father through. With every absurd reveal, Gethard masterfully navigates the improv to allow Lapkus to shine, reveling in the most unbelievable of storylines, while he remains the perfect, grounded foil. His ability to play the show straight ends up being equally as hilarious as Lapkus’ elaborately sadistic character.</p>
